Audit Trail's Usefulness

According to Investopia, an audit trail is defined as ‘a step-by-step record by which accounting or trade data can be traced to its source.’ (James Chen 2019, p.1). Simply stating, it is our digital footprint on the internet. It holds a record of our history, including what we do, who we interact with and so on. GitHub’s audit trail works in the same way, which means it records our activities on GitHub and with it, a lot of benefits come along. Because we can trace back to our history, it is very easy to get a grasp of what had been done and what had not, hence making a plan for today’s work. We can also have a control over our projects and manages the changes, especially in a group project where there are many parties involved. With the audit trail, me and my partner were able to resolve conflicts nicely and it also allowed us to work better as a team.
